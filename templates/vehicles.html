@0 Kilometers

~Engine Start & Idle (Before Driving)

~The car burns fuel while idling, producing ~0.2 kg CO₂ per minute.

~Assuming 30 seconds of idling: ~0.1 kg CO₂ emitted

@1 Kilometers

~More fuel is burned due to acceleration, emitting 0.2 kg CO₂.

~Tire wear releases microplastics into the air and water.

~Road dust and particulate matter increase, affecting air quality.

@3 Kilometers (Constant speed)

~The car reaches fuel-efficient cruising mode.

~Emissions stabilize at ~0.18 kg CO₂ per km.

~Total emissions so far: ~0.65 kg CO₂.

@5 Kilometers

~If in stop-and-go traffic, extra fuel is burned.

~Braking releases fine particles (PM2.5, PM10) into the air.

~Estimated CO₂ emissions: ~1.0 kg CO₂ total.

@7 Kilometers

~Engine is at full operating temperature; peak NOx and CO₂ emissions.

~Exhaust heat contributes to urban heat islands.

~Total CO₂ emitted: ~1.5 kg CO₂.

@10 Kilometers

~The full ride has released ~1.8 kg CO₂ into the atmosphere.

~Engine cools, releasing residual heat into the environment.

~Brake dust, tire wear, and road debris remain as pollutants.
